Introduction


“Tell me and I forget, teach me and I may remember, involve me and I learn” (Benjamin Franklin).

According to the above quotation of Benjamin Franklin, telling someone something is fleeting, teaching someone something is more valuable, and learning something is unforgettable. If we consider Benjamin's quote, it meets all the educational needs of the twenty-first century, because A 21st-century education does not entail a class of students sitting silently in neat rows at their desks, copying what the teacher says or writes on the chalkboard (or smartboard). It's a little more difficult to describe and demonstrate 21st-century learning. It's a little more challenging. It's a little more sophisticated this time. It's a lot more difficult to evaluate. It fosters learning settings where students are actively shaping their learning. And to achieve this target the role of educators is very much important. Educators' responsibility in the twenty-first century must be to assist every student in learning how to learn. It encourages creativity, collaborates, expects, and rewards critical thinking, and teaches children not only how to communicate, but also how to communicate effectively. These are the abilities that students will need to succeed in today's and tomorrow's dynamic workplaces. To accomplish the aims of the 21st-century education system, we as teachers must be adaptive to new transitions then only, we can build a knowledgeable society.

My role as a teacher will be to encourage my students to be creative, to allow them to display their work in a variety of ways, to encourage them to think critically to solve challenges, and to protect their emotional and moral values as well. I will make every effort to create a welcoming environment for all my students to make them feel secure.

This PG diploma enabled me to learn a lot of new things, such as different technology, teaching styles, classroom management, and most importantly, the practicum course, which provided me with extremely beneficial training and enhanced my teaching abilities, which would undoubtedly benefit me as a teacher.

Mehvish Tanveer Winter 2021
