Online Lesson Teaching


I had the opportunity to design two lesson plans in this course, and I also had the opportunity to present this lesson to my other colleagues in online classrooms. Because it was my first time teaching online, it was a huge challenge for me. However, these online teaching experiences have broadened my understanding because I learned so many various things from my colleagues who had prior expertise. My coworkers were supportive, and even though it was tough for me to present my first Grade 5 class in front of an audience of adult professionals, my coworkers actively engaged in my classes.

During my online lesson teaching, I faced the following challenges:

  • One of the major difficulties I came up with as a teacher was, sometimes your students do not respond and Itâ€™s difficult to know whether your students' lack of response is due to a lack of understanding, a lack of focus, or simply a fear of answering. You can either keep exploring with more questions.
  • It's not simple to keep your students engaged and motivated. That has always been the case, and now that they are learning from home rather than in a real classroom, the issue becomes even greater. Keeping my pupils happy, engaged, and motivated had been a major challenge.
  • When you can't see your students, it's impossible to judge their understanding, and it can make a teacher feel insecure.
  • You can't make a breakout room for young students because you don't know if they'll show up and engage in the group project.

Types of Sentences was the first lesson I gave to grade 5 pupils. To convey my lecture, I used Ahaslide. I attempted to involve my students as much as possible, but because it was my first lecture, I made a lot of mistakes. My professor's feedback on my first lesson offered me a fresh perspective on how to prepare for my second lesson, and thanks to that, my second lesson plan went very well.

My second lesson was about verbs for 3 grade. I used so many acidities in my lesson to engage my students actively in it. I use Nearpod to deliver my lesson. I also used VR Reality in it. I prepared the Time to Climb game as an exit ticket for my young learners. I introduced wheels of name to choose students randomly for participating in the lesson.
