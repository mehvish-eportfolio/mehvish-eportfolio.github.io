Lesson Observations


Peer observation enhances communication skills. Being an observer allows you the opportunity to discuss teaching and learning, as well as practice giving constructive feedback to your colleagues. No matter what type of lesson you observed, whether good or terrible, both provided you with a positive experience. Because you can take up all the useful concepts that you want to incorporate in your teaching from a good lesson, and you can learn what you don't need to do in your class from a terrible session.

I picked up so many beneficial ideas from observing other colleagues that I can use in my teaching because it provided me with new ways to approach a problem that would help me to establish new strategies for handling my students according to their specific needs.

I had an opportunity to observe two lessons during my practicum course. Both lessons were video recordings of my other coworkers. My first lesson observation was for an eighth-grade science class. The second was an English lesson for first graders. Both lessons provided me with a positive learning experience. And most importantly it served as a useful preparation for my two online teaching lessons.
